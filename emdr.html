Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)

EMDR is an evidence-based psychotherapy that helps people heal from trauma and distressing life experiences. It uses guided eye movements to help process and integrate difficult memories, reducing their emotional charge.

How Does EMDR Work?

  • Identifies troubling memories or beliefs.
  • Processes these using bilateral stimulation (such as guided eye movements).
  • Helps the brain reprocess the memory, reducing distress and enabling positive beliefs.

Benefits of EMDR

  • Effective for trauma, PTSD, anxiety, grief, and more
  • Does not require detailed discussion of the distressing event
  • Often produces results more quickly than traditional therapy
